Coleslaw recipe

coleslaw, a tasty and healthy side dish

Coleslaw is a classic side dish made of shredded cabbage and carrots, mixed with a creamy or tangy dressing. It offers a refreshing and crunchy texture, often complementing main courses like barbecue, fried chicken, or sandwiches. This versatile salad provides a balance of flavors, making it a popular addition to a wide range of meals.

Ingredients

  • Shredded cabbage
  • Shredded carrots
  • Mayonnaise
  • Vinegar (white or apple cider)
  • Sugar
  • Salt
  • Black pepper

steps

  1. Shred the cabbage and carrots using a grater or a food processor.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the shredded cabbage and carrots.
  3. In a separate bowl, prepare the dressing by mixing mayonnaise, vinegar, sugar, salt, and black pepper. Adjust the dressing's sweetness and tanginess to your preference.
  4. Pour the dressing over the shredded vegetables and toss until they are evenly coated.
  5. Optional: Add in any extra ingredients like chopped onions, thinly sliced bell peppers, chopped fresh parsley, grated apples, or pineapple chunks for additional flavor and texture.
  6. Refrigerate the coleslaw for at least 30 minutes before serving to allow the flavors to meld together and the coleslaw to chill.
  7. Serve the coleslaw as a refreshing side dish alongside your favorite main courses.
